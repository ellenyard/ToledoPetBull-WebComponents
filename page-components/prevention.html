Spay/Neuter Support

We help families cover the cost of spay/neuter surgery to prevent overpopulation.

How it works: Submit an application to join our waiting list. We'll contact you with next steps.

Pet Food Pantry

On the 2nd and 4th Tuesday of every month, we provide dog and cat food to Lucas County residents facing financial hardship.

Eligibility: All pets in household must be spayed/neutered, or you must be willing to have it done. Bring photo ID and proof of income. Confirm date and time on our calendar.

Free Dog Training & Vaccine Clinics

Once a month, we bring our services to you with free rabies and DHPP vaccines for dogs, free dog training, and free resources.
