Good Manners 1: Foundations

$80 | 5-week series
Build the foundation skills every dog needs while learning how to communicate clearly and train effectively.

Note: First session is for humans only. Not suitable for dogs with aggression or reactivity.

Dogs and handlers learn the basic behaviors that create a polite and responsive companion.

  • Name recognition and attention to handler
  • Sit and down
  • Stay
  • Beginning loose leash walking
  • Beginner recall (coming when called)
  • Polite greetings introduction

Focus: Building strong engagement, communication, and basic manners that prepare dogs for the next level of training.

Good Manners 2: Smart Dog

$80 | 4-week series
Build your dog's focus, confidence, and impulse control through fun training games and problem-solving activities.

Prerequisites: Completion of Good Manners 1 required.

Dogs practice their foundation behaviors while learning to think, focus on their handler, and work through new challenges.

  • Focus and engagement games
  • Recall games that strengthen coming when called
  • Loose leash walking games
  • Impulse control exercises
  • Place or settle on a mat
  • Beginner scent and problem-solving games

Focus: Building a dog that thinks, listens, and stays connected to their handler, even with distractions.

Good Manners 3: Advanced Skills

$80 | 4-week series
Strengthen the skills learned in earlier classes, helping your dog respond consistently even with distractions.

Prerequisites: Completion of Good Manners 2 required.

We build on your dog's foundation and begin practicing behaviors with greater duration, distance, and real-world challenges.

  • Longer sit, down, and stay
  • Loose leash walking with distractions
  • Stronger recall (coming when called)
  • Leave it with real temptations
  • Polite greetings with people

Focus: Building reliability through duration, distance, and distractions.

Next Class: Check back soon!

Good Manners 4: Real Life Skills

$80 | 4-week series
Apply your training to everyday situations with practical, real-world practice.

Prerequisites: Completion of Good Manners 3 required.

Dogs learn to apply their training to everyday situations and real-world challenges.

  • Walking past other dogs
  • Greeting people politely
  • Relaxing in public
  • Ignoring food and distractions
  • Longer stays
  • Recall around real-life challenges

Focus: Real-world situations.
